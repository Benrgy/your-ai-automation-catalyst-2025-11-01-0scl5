4. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They allow websites to recognize you and remember information about your visit, such as your preferences and login information.

Types of Cookies We Use

We use the following types of cookies on our website:

  • Essential Cookies: These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly and enable core functionality such as security and network management. These cookies cannot be disabled.
  • Performance Cookies: These cookies collect information about how you use our website, such as which pages you visit most often and whether you receive error messages. This information helps us improve the website's performance.
  • Functionality Cookies: These cookies remember choices you make on our website, such as your language preferences or login information, to provide a more personalized experience.
  • Marketing Cookies: These cookies track your activity across websites to display targeted advertisements based on your interests and browsing behavior.

Other Tracking Technologies

In addition to cookies, we use other tracking technologies including:

  • Web Beacons: Small graphics files used to track website activity and user behavior
  • Pixels: Tiny images embedded in web pages and emails to track interactions
  • Local Storage: Browser technology that stores data locally on your device

Managing Your Cookie Preferences

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can set your browser to refuse cookies or alert you when cookies are being sent. However, please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of our website and your ability to access certain features. For more information about managing cookies, please visit www.aboutcookies.org or www.allaboutcookies.org.
